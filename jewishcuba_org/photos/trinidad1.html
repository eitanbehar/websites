A young child who lived somewhere near the old abandoned church above Trinidad.



In an area of about ten square blocks in Trinidad the buildings are painted and cared for.

School children in uniform. The red scarves represent the communist party and the "revolution."


A typical street near Trinidad's tourist area.


This shy young woman sells bean necklaces and dolls her aunt makes.
She was not aware I was taking this photo.



Cuba's children: confident, brash, polite most of the time. Most seem to have a great sense of humor and fun.

One stopped to talk to one of the ever-present children.
