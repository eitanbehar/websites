HERMANDAD LUKUMI

"Donation of Medicine Right to the Hands of the Cuban People"

Existing Situation

Religious organizations donating medicine in Cuba give a large portion to the government. Only a small part of the medicine reaches the people in need. In many instances the donations serve as tools intended to draw converts. The government in turn keeps a portion for free distribution and the other is sold to supply the needs of tourist.

Millions of dollars worth of medicine are sent yearly from family members in the exile community. They pay the high cost of medicine at their local pharmacy. To ship the medicine they must go to a licensed agency and pay up to fifteen ($15.00) dollars per pound. United States law limits the shipments of medicine to two-hundred ($200) dollars monthly. Another option is to send limited US dollars and the relative in Cuba can try to buy his or her needs from government stores.

CLBA Alternative Intent

Hermandad Lukumi is a Church of the Lukumi Babalu Aye (CLBA) humanitarian relief program. CLBA is the religious organization promoting the donation of medicine to family members in the exile community. Joining our program are numerous nongovernmental charities in Europe. This is an apolitical independent Lukumi program for humanitarian relief purposes only. CLBA will maintain that all donations are to be delivered directly to needy recipients through the petition of a family member in the USA and abroad.

The system of delivery is courier service. All licensed courier agencies to Cuba can participate in this program to implement delivery procedure. Approval of donations is a sole right of CLBA. Participating agencies must maintain a fixed shipping rate of $7.00 per pound without any additional charges. Agencies and CLBA guarantee 100% replacement of donation(s) and shipping charge(s) in cases of lost delivery.

CLBA will maintain a humanist, not discriminatory policy on donations. Donations are to be given directly into the hands of every recipient regardless of religious belief or atheist. There will be no political slogans or religious propaganda seeking converts using techniques for psychological coercion.
