B'NAI B'RITH ANNOUNCES NEW COMMITTEE TO HELP JEWS OF CUBA

AFTER MISSION TO CUBA, PARTICIPANTS VOW TO HELP COMMUNITY

Washington, D.C. (February 12, 1997) -- Armed with suitcases overflowing with heart medications, vitamins, cough syrups, and other medicines, 32 people from across North America participated in a B'nai B'rith humanitarian mission to Cuba seeking to help the Jewish community physically and spiritually. The participants came back with a grim report. Medicines, food (including milk) and clothes are either non-existent or are unaffordable. The children are in particular need. They are not allowed any milk after two years of age, with the exception of a medical emergency, and are in desperate need of vitamins. One shirt costs one month's salary.

Six of the participants were born in Cuba including Michael Mandel, a cantorial student who performed several Judaic concerts for the culturally-starved community accompanied by Cuban-born Ruth Padorr, now a businesswoman in Chicago.

After returning from the recent trip and seeing the lack of medical supplies and food available, B'nai B'rith International has formed a new committee to provide medicines, clothing and educational materials on an ongoing basis to the 1,600-person Cuban Jewish community and to inform Jews worldwide of their plight.

Richard D. Heideman, international chairman of B'nai B'rith's Center For Public Policy, has selected Elizabeth (Betty) Baer chairperson of the new B'nai B'rith Committee on Cuban Affairs. Baer is a paralegal from Richmond, and is the wife of B'nai B'rith's international president, Tommy P. Baer.

"In keeping with B'nai B'rith's 153 year tradition of providing aid to Jews throughout the world, we have created this special committee to assist in improving the lives of this isolated community who are in desperate need of even the basic necessities of life," said Heideman. Heideman also appointed Irving Silver, an attorney from Mobile, Alabama, the Vice Chairman of the Committee on Cuban Affairs. Silver is the former chairman of the Center for Public Policy.

"We plan to obtain and send educational and religious materials written in Spanish and sponsor more humanitarian missions," said Mrs. Baer. "The needs are immense."

The Maimonedes Lodge of B'nai B'rith was founded in Havana in 1943. Strengthening the programmatic relationship with this lodge will be another goal of the committee. B'nai B'rith is in a unique position to aid the Jews of Cuba because of its worldwide membership and its Latin American units throughout the U.S.
