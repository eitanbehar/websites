The Great Jewish-Cuban Bicycle Tour of 2004
(A proposed charitable and exploratory trip)

(Photos courtesy of Barbara and Wallace Smith, www.bicyclingcuba.com)

Preliminary list of questions and answers

Will this be a legally licensed trip?

Yes. This trip is for members of B'nai B'rith and we will be traveling on their license. You can join from their website.

Will we be involved in charitable activities?

Yes. We will deliver medical, educational, religious, and other supplies to different congregations. We will also help in small fix-up projects as needed.

What is the time period for this trip?

The month of February 2004.

How many people will be going on this trip?

No more than four or five. This is an exploratory trip to get firsthand information on the needs of the separate Jewish congregations, to provide some basic support through charitable donations, and to develop a schedule for future trips. With the short notice time I don't anticipate many people will be going.

Can I just go for part of the trip?

Yes. You can go for any amount of time you wish. The participating travel agency will know our schedule and help you make contact and arrange transportation.

This sounds a little informal and unorganized. Aren't all the arrangements made for participants?

No. This is not a trip for those looking for comfort, pre-booked lodging or meals, fixed schedules, and planned social activities. This is a trip for veteran travelers who have a great sense of adventure, who don't mind occasional discomfort, who are in moderately good physical condition, and who have a sense of humor.

What is the tentative itinerary?

Initially to fly into Havana, then to Santiago de Cuba. Due to the prevailing winds we will ride from east to west. See the map below for a tentative list of congregations we will visit.

How much will this trip cost?

You will pay only your actual travel costs. There are no "administrative" fees, since you will do much of your own organizing. In addition to airfare and taxes, you should plan on from $15 to $30 per day lodging, and around $20 per day for meals. Bicycle repair, tips, gifts, entertainment, etc., will be extra and at your discretion.

Can I bring my own bike or can I rent one in Cuba?

Either. The participating travel agency will have bikes you can rent in Cuba. The airline flying from Miami (recommended) will............

What's biking in Cuba like?

Here are two good online resources: www.bicyclingcuba.com and "Viva Cuba! An 1800 mile bike trip in the Caribbean." If you are interested in an excellent description and more details, buy the book by Barbara and Wallace Smith (no relationship to me).

Where do we get the material to be donated? Do we have to buy things?

We have the materials to be donated.
