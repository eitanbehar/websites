RECENT CONTAINERS SENT TO CUBA

We’re very happy with the work we’ve been able to do recently, but at
the moment all of that is overwhelmed by the work we need to do as a
result of hurricane Dennis.

But still, it’s worth recounting some recent successes.

First, we are VERY EXCITED about our recent partnership with
WORKING BIKES COOP of Chicago. We recently went out there to learn how they
operate and they are wonderful

It works like this. Working Bikes goes to the scrap yard in Chicago
where metal is collected and melted down every day. ‘Junqueros’ (mostly
Latino) drive up in trucks with all kinds of metal trash, and the
Working Bikes guys flag down any truck with a bicycle on it, and buy the
bicycle for a few dollars. All of these bicycles are repaired, and some of
them are sold inexpensively, which provides the money to ship the other
bicycles all over the world.

Thanks to our relationship with Working Bikes, and with the Baracoa/
St. Augustine Friendship Association, we have sent about a thousand
bicycles to Cuba, primarily to handicapped people who can ride bicycles,
and to hospital workers- from doctors to cooks to the people who clean
the floors. Everyone who works in a Cuban hospital is doing heroic duty,
and deserves a small reward.

And now we have convinced Working Bikes to collect wheelchairs as
well as Bicycles! We have send about 500 wheelchairs, and we are still
collecting about 10 a week.

Specifically, in the last three months we have sent two containers of
bicycles and wheelchairs to Oriente-Guantanamo, Santiago de Cuba,
Baracoa, and Yateras (in Guantanamo Province.) Another container is on the
way.

Thanks to our partnerships with the One World Foundation, we are
sending an additional container, mostly disposable surgical supplies, to
Santiago de Cuba. Thanks to our partnership with the Madison Medical
Project, we are helping to send a container of medicine and supplies to
Camaguey.

We are well on our way to meeting our goal of 12 containers sent
this year.

HURRICANE RELIEF

Hurricane Dennis has struck Cuba very, very hard.

We are working with our existing partners, and some new partners, to
send additional containers of Hurricane relief to Cuba. In addition to
the always needed medicine and surgical supplies, we are going to send
household items such as mattresses, cooking pots, disinfectant,
detergent, just about anything, because everything is needed by the tens of
thousands of people who lost everything they had. JUST TODAY WE LOADED A
40 FOOT CONTAINER IN MADISON AND CHICAGO WITH MATTRESSES, WHEELCHAIRS,
AND DISPOSABLE HOSPITAL SUPPLIES, DESTINATION GRANMA AND SANTIAGO.

Although we are still working out the details, it is likely that
distribution will be through ACLIFIM (association of the handicapped) and the
Jewish Synagogue in Santiago de Cuba, working through the very small
Jewish communities in Campechuela and Manzanillo. Our Jewish friends in Cuba
are a reliable and appropriate NGO who will help supervise the massive work that we are
going to initiate, and help us distribute the materials to the entire community.

In order to document this emergency hurricane relief, we have created a
new web page at www.cubacaribe.com . We will list every donation
(unless a donor wants to be anonymous) not only of money but also every
bottle of aspirin, every pot and pan, and we will document the distribution
of everything as well. Our intention is to be 100% transparent in
everything we do. ALL money donated for hurricane relief will go towards
shipping supplies- NO money will go towards salary, administrative
expenses, phone bills- NOTHING. And we will document that.

AND HERE IS SOME GREAT NEWS- WE JUST RECEIVED A MATCHING CHALLENGE
DONATION FROM A GOOD FRIEND IN CALIFORNIA- ALL DONATIONS UP TO A TOTAL OF
$1,250 RECEIVED IN THE NEXT 10 DAYS WILL BE MATCHED.

If you want to help, we have set up a Paypal account to make this as
easy as possible. It costs about $25 to send a wheelchair to a kid who
right now cannot get across the room. For $100 we can put a few
families back on their feet. You can make the difference with your donation.
Please help.


SMALL CONFERENCE FOR CUBA RELATED HUMANITARIAN ORGANIZATIONS

We always like to partner with other organizations. There are so many
good people who want to do good work. Therefore our friends at the
Vermont Institute on Cuba are sponsoring a small conference for sister
organizations, October 1-2 in Burlington Vermont. This is a WORKING GROUP
for organizations that want to learn more about the Commerce license,
OFAC travel license, resolving problems in the USA and in Cuba. Invitees
include the Cuba Aids Project, the St. Augustine Baracoa Friendship
Association, The Wisconsin Medical Project, The Cuba--America Jewish
Mission, the One World Foundation, Infomed, the Dubois Family Foundation,
Marazul Travel, and many others.

If you know organizations that have a Commerce/and or OFAC license,
are currently engaged in humanitarian work in Cuba, or are interested in
doing this work, please let them know of this opportunity to share and
learn to do more.

TRAVEL TO CUBA

CMT can authorize professional and technical specialists who wish to
travel to Cuba on a full time schedule of activities directly incident to
sending more containers under our Commerce license. We can send people
in groups, or in some cases, we can send qualified individuals
traveling on their own.

In additional to the ‘ordinary’ trips to send ‘ordinary’ supplies,
we are going to lead a few trips specifically designed to help us send
more emergency hurricane relief. Please write if you would like to know
if you are qualified to participate in these trips.
